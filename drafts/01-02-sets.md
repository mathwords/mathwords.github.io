**I.1   Sets**



---

***Exercise 4***

Since $X$ and $Y$ are non-empty, the statements $u \in X$ and $v \in Y$ are true for some $u$ and $v$. We can then reason as follows:
$$
(u \in X) \and (v \in Y) \iff  (u, v) \in X \times Y \iff (u, v) \in Y \times X \\
\iff (u \in Y) \and (v \in X)
$$
Therefore, $X = Y$.
